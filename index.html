Calculating the Probablility of an Online Sale Using the Logit Model

Ron Lyles Jr
ECON 495
Fall 2018

Overview:

  • Log of web traffic to Google's Merch store
  • September 2016 - January 2017
  • 903,653 observations
  • Goal: Find impact of different factors on likelihood of making a purchase

Prior Work

  • "Determinants of Recent Online Purchasing and the Percentage of Income spent online"
    • Hannah & Lybecker, 2010. Internation Business Research
  • "Online Shopping Acceptance Model â€” A Critical Survey of Consumer Factors in Online Shopping" (2007)
    • Zhou, Dai, & Zhang, 2007. Journal of Electronic Commerce Research

Models

  • Logit model for each factor
  • Dependent variable: whether or not visitor made a purchase
  • Marginal effects will be reported
  • Three categories of factors:
    • Location
    • Marketing
    • User

Methodology:

  • For each category, choose factors that have at least 10 sales
  • "A Simulation Study of the Number of Events per Variable in Logistic Regression Analysis" Pedduzi et. all (1996)
  • "Relaxing the Rule of Ten Events per Variable in Logistic and Cox Regression" Vittinghoff and McCulloch (2007)
